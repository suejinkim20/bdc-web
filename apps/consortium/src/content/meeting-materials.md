## Overview

The NHLBI BioData Catalyst (BDC)® March 2025 Bi-Annual Meeting was held virtually via Microsoft Teams on **March 3-4, 2025** bringing together key stakeholders, researchers, and technical teams to discuss ongoing projects, recent advancements, and strategic priorities. Across both days this meeting was attended by 112 individuals.

**Day 1** of this meeting featured updates on key initiatives to expand BDC’s footprint in the NHLBI research community. These updates focused on addressing feedback from recent engagements with the TOPMed community, as well as hearing updates on consortium efforts to build the ecosystem’s capabilities via migration of the TOPMed Exchange Area, expanding BDC’s AI capabilities, and three ARPA-H BDF initiatives which heavily feature BDC in their research. Additionally, this day saw the meeting attendees splitting up into breakout sessions to discuss high-value topics to project leadership and development.**Day 2** featured an emphasis in hearing updates from the BDC Data Management Core (DMC), NHLBI ECO team, and BDC3 User Engagement team on their combined efforts within the last six months. This day also featured a debrief from BDC3 on the outcomes of the Imaging Tiger Team, as well as an update from NHLBI staff on the recent changes to the NIH Genomic Data Sharing Policy (and the potential positive impacts to BDC growth and usage), as well as a refresher presentation on empowering “Apps” to be brought into the BDC Ecosystem by researchers.

## Overview

The NHLBI BioData Catalyst (BDC)® September 2025 Bi-Annual Meeting was held in-person at NHLBI’s Rockledge campus and virtually, via Microsoft Teams, on **September 16-17, 2025**, bringing together key stakeholders, researchers, and technical teams to discuss ongoing projects, recent advancements, and strategic priorities. Across both days, 123 individuals attended.**Day 1** centered on NHLBI’s evolving priorities and BioData Catalyst’s central role within the broader NIH data ecosystem. Updates included NHLBI’s Precision Medicine and Data Science Strategic Vision Implementation Plan (PMDS SVIP) goals, the NHLBI AI program, and the BDC Priorities Roadmap, alongside progress in TOPMed Exchange Area migration and RECOVER data integration as models for large-scale, high-priority initiatives. The BDC Data Management Core showcased the improved efficiency of Data Harmonization and previews of upcoming ecosystem integrations from the Harmonized Data Model’s Phase 1 advances, while Velsera shared progress on expanding imaging workflows. Breakout sessions covered: A) TOPMed User Consultation and direct engagement on increasing BDC touchpoints and B) BDC Developers discussing technical issues. **Day 2** featured progress updates, community insights, and forward-looking strategies to strengthen BDC. ARPA-H BDF performers shared timelines for wrapping up current iterations, with RENCI and Geneial concluding work in September 2025 and Gen3 expected to finish in December. The Growth & Retention team highlighted ecosystem usage growth, despite external challenges, and shared lessons from diverse research community engagement, underscoring the need for a standardized BDC Playbook to guide evolving user needs. AI initiative and imaging data ingest presentations demonstrated technical advances and sustainability challenges, while RAS integration planning emphasized BDC’s role as an early adopter, shaping NIH-wide implementation. NHLBI and EEP feedback encouraged greater focus on metrics, transparency, and showcasing real-world use cases, while a Fireside Chat celebrated milestones achieved, emphasized the importance of strategic planning and reinforced momentum toward positioning BDC as a “first-in-class” research community ecosystem.

## Overview

The NHLBI BioData Catalyst (BDC)® September 2024 Quarterly Meeting was held virtually via Zoom on **September 10-11, 2024**. The event was attended by 82 people total across the two days and included the BDC consortium members, NHLBI staff, and External Expert Panel (EEP) members.

The main goals for this meeting were to **(A)** review recent wins for the consortium, both in advances made in the ingest of Imaging Data, AI integration, and the user journey, **(B)** announce the successful launch of the interim BioData Catalyst website and a demo of it’s content, and **(C)** introduce the new ARPA-H BDF performer teams along with a review of their scope of work.

The meeting was kicked off with housekeeping items, including welcoming our new External Expert Panel (EEP) member, Marylyn Ritchie, and reminding attendees of the outcomes from our previous June QM and work done to date on appropriate action items.

**Day 1** focused on a series of presentations and discussions introducing the new ARPA-H Biomedical Data Fabric (BDF) project teams, including Geneial, Verily, RENCI, and The University of Chicago, and a review of their proposed scope of work. We also received updates on imaging data ingest, and we reviewed the current AI and machine learning capabilities on BioData Catalyst, highlighting the applicability of these technologies to multimodal data use cases. The Growth and Retention team presented how BDC3 analyzes user investigator journeys to understand user needs better and tailor the BDC experience.

**Day 2** started with announcing the interim BioData Catalyst website, demoing diverse content and usage. Then, we heard updates from the Dug team on advancements of the Dug Semantic Search, with improved functionality and external integrations. The second day continued with a presentation from the DMC on its unified data model and how they are continuing to refine this collaborative process, which will include data ingestions, mapping strategies, and scaling up the curations process. We also heard from an expert about the importance of flexible data harmonization tools like LinkML to better meet researchers’ needs.

The meeting concluded with debriefs from the EEP members and NHLBI representatives, who praised progress in AI/LLM developments, interoperability efforts, and platform maturity, with excitement around future AI capabilities. They also acknowledged the team’s communication and metric development improvements, along with website advancements. The NHLBI fireside discussion brought further praise for BDC’s organization, a welcome to the new ARPA-H projects, and the evolving data model, signaling a solid momentum as we head into the next phase.

## Overview

The meeting concluded with debriefs from the EEP members and NHLBI representatives where they highlighted some of the challenges users may face with the BioData Catalyst ecosystem, but also praised the project’s efforts in using FHIR for EHR data and leveraging LLMs for innovative solutions. The strong community outreach efforts were also praised, and several actionable recommendations were provided for the future of BDC.

The BioData Catalyst Coordinating Center was proud to host the sixth BioData Catalyst Face-to-Face meeting in Chapel Hill, NC on March 5 & 6, 2020. 46 BDCatalyst consortium and NHLBI members participated in-person with an additional 18 virtual attendees.

**Day 1**set expectations with highlighting the great work and progress achieved by the consortium to-date, before shifting focus to the high priority items and near-term deliverables; introduction of a new Change Control Board (CCB) and Change Management Framework, establishing the Data Release Management Working Group (DRMWG), Data Management Plan development, engaging and understanding Fellows needs, as well as realigning and prioritizing Q2 deliverables. "Life after go-live" was discussed followed by updates from the Fellows Program, CICI, User Services, BDCatalyst Interoperation, and a presentation by the VA on "Bridging Data Silos Without Breaking Them." Day 1 concluded by reviewing updates to the Strategic and Implementation Plans, a Day 1 recap and preview for Day 2.

**Day 2**saw heavy modification to the agenda (a growing F2F trend) with a focus on prioritizing Q2 deliverables. The inaugural DRMWG discussion resulted in prioritizing: 1) need to understand Fellows projects, 2) identification of selected TAR files, and 3) dependent on #1, Freeze 8 necessary files data for Fellows. Working Groups met to review/revise their charters and reported back to the consortium on their progress. Main takeaways were the urgent need to standup the new working group framework and organizational structure. The timely identification and response of and to risks grows all the more important with the onboarding of Fellows and early users.

The University of California, Santa Cruz (Team Calcium) hosted the fifth BioData Catalyst Face-to-Face meeting in Santa Cruz, CA on December 9 & 10, 2019. 52 BDCatalyst consortium and NHLBI members participated in-person with additional virtual attendees.

**Day 1** began with team introductions and goals for the December F2F, with our meeting goal being _Stayin' Go Live_. Our top objective: to stay on track, in unison, for Go Live. After the success of Boston's F2F, the consortium then split again into 2 large breakout sessions: our PI Breakout and our Developers Go Lively.

Session One of the day split into two groups, one reviewing "PIC-SURE (an example of the API running on a Jupyter notebook on Seven Bridges" (presented by Paul Avillach and coordinated by Alison Leaf), and the second group reviewing "GWAS on Terra" (presented by Alex Waldrop and coordinated by Beth Sheets).

After lunch, consortium members returned to plenary for a session of o16n LIVE! led by Brandi Davis-Dusenberry and Bob Grossman.

Session Two contained two breakouts, one for "Gen3 User Access" (led by Garrett Rupp, Alex VanTol, Brian Hannafious) and another for "Aggregating and filtering variant annotations for multiple variant" GWAS (led by Deepti Jain and coordinated by Alison Leaf).

All sessions and breakouts for the day then provided report backs.

The BioData Catalyst Coordinating Center (BDC3) gave three presentation updates for the projects it is currently facilitating: BioData Catalyst Fellows Program; User Services; and Cloud IC Interoperability.

**Day 2** saw heavy modification to the agenda after the dialogue and progress made during Day 1. The meeting opened with a frank conversation about the considerations for Go Live, and what meeting attendees could agree were the minimum requirements for Go Live. Additional discussion included external community pressures, data availability, creation of a dependency map, and development of the BDC "search suite".

The second day also held the working group breakouts, including Tools and Apps, User Engagement, and Data Harmonization.

The consortium regrouped for a group session on Tutorial Development led by the BDC3. After lunch, Stan Ahalt led our Release 2 Plenary Discussion: "Rocks in the Riverbed". The meeting concluded with final thoughts from Jon Kaltman, Alastair Thomson, David Mendelson, and Ingrid Borecki.

Overall, the meeting concluded with very concrete decisions about what should and should not block Go Live, and what action was needed by all teams to move towards a more unified vision of the final BioData Catalyst Ecosystem.

NHLBI hosted the third DataSTAGE Face-to-Face meeting in Bethesda, MD on June 4 & 5, 2019. Sixty-two STAGE consortium and NHLBI members participated in-person with additional virtual attendees.

The remainder of the day involved reviewing and rethinking Workplan 3.0 User Narratives. It was kicked off with some NHLBI guidance, after which breakout groups were formed around UN themes: Users/Training/Production, Analysis, & Interconnecting.

On October 17, 2018, 23 DataSTAGE consortia & NHLBI representatives gathered before the DCPPC F2F meeting to demo recent activities.

An overview from Steering Committee Chair, Ingrid Borecki, STAGECC PI, Stan Ahalt, and STAGECC Co-PI, Becky Boyles preceded the 3 demo presentations, given by the Full Stack Plus teams.
